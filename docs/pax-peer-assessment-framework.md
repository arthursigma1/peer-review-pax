# PAX Peer Assessment Framework

Status: PAX-specific business spec. This document captures the commissioning
team's intended workflow, output shape, and initial value-driver hypotheses for
Patria runs. It is a required starting point for PAX-specific VDA work, but it
is not the statistical rulebook and it does not override the repository
methodology.

## 1. Purpose

Use this document to keep the PAX work aligned with the original business
intent:

1. screen a broad peer set using business and financial indicators tied to
   valuation multiples
2. select approximately 9-12 peers for strategic deep-dives
3. turn those findings into practical guidance for PAX at both platform and
   business-unit level

The desired output is a management instrument for PHL, the CEO, and BU heads,
not a neutral peer memo.

## 2. Workplan Flow

### Stage 1. Overview and Peers Prioritization

Required outcomes:

- business and financial metrics comparison across the peer universe
- valuation multiple and value-driver correlation analysis
- initial testing of the key indicator hypotheses in Section 3
- peer-set selection for deep-dive, targeting approximately 9-12 GPs

### Stage 2. Selected Peers' Strategy Deep-Dive

Required outcomes:

- deep-dive each selected peer's strategic agenda and business value drivers
- use the PAX peer strategy ontology in
  `docs/pax-peer-strategy-ontology.md`
- explain how each peer's business-model design appears to produce the observed
  KPI outcomes
- compare rule-confirming cases and instructive exceptions

### Stage 3. Lessons Learned and Opportunities

Required outcomes:

- industry value-creation principles and foundations
- clear takeaways under three buckets:
  - reinforce current strategies
  - bring additional angles and opportunities
  - suggest new initiatives
- ranked strategic implications for PAX valuation

## 3. Initial Value-Driver Hypotheses

These are required starting hypotheses for PAX runs. They must be tested,
classified, and either supported, downgraded, or rejected based on evidence.
They are not confirmed truths.

### EPS / DE-share

Test at minimum:

- nominal level
- growth

### FEAUM

Test at minimum:

- size
- growth
- net organic growth
- diversification

### AUM and Mix

Test at minimum:

- product mix
- geographic mix

### Market Position

Test at minimum:

- TAM
- market growth
- market share

### Business Efficiency

Test at minimum:

- ROA
- FRE growth
- FRE margin

### Market-Structure Context

These should be considered at least as contextual metrics:

- average daily trading volume
- passive ownership share

## 4. Source Expectations

The deep-dive stage should prioritize:

1. 20-F and equivalent filings
2. management presentations
3. Investor Day materials
4. quarterly reports and earnings materials
5. research reports
6. interviews

Other credible sources may be added when they improve evidence quality.

## 5. Output Expectations by Audience

### Platform / PHL Level

Must answer:

- which drivers appear to matter most for valuation
- which strategic configurations are associated with those drivers
- what PAX should reinforce, add, or avoid
- which medium-term priorities deserve management attention

### Business-Unit Level

Must answer:

- what specifically mattered inside each relevant asset class
- which peer patterns are transferable
- which moves are not transferable
- what the BU head should treat as do's and don'ts

## 6. Usage Rules

- Mandatory starting point for PAX-specific runs
- Not a closed ontology and not a closed metric list
- Agents may add dimensions, metrics, or hypotheses when evidence warrants
- Agents may reject an initial hypothesis when evidence does not support it
- Statistical classification, report governance, and hard-fail rules remain
  governed by `docs/pax-first-valuation-driver-methodology.md`
