CEO ALEX SAIGH — PREPARED REMARKS:

OVERALL FRAMING:
"Very excited to report our 4th quarter results, a capstone to a very successful 2025."
Patria is positioned to "achieve, and hopefully exceed, the three-year fundraising and FRE objectives" established at the December 2024 Investor Day.

RECORD RESULTS:
- Organic fundraising: $7.7B (record, exceeded $6.6B revised target by >$1B)
- FRE: $202.5M (+19% YoY), FRE margin 58.9%
- Q4 FRE: $64.3M (+17% YoY), FRE margin 63.6%
- FEAUM: $40.8B (+24% YoY)
- Total AUM: $52.6B (+26% YoY)
- DE full year: $200.9M ($1.27/share)
- Q4 DE: $78.5M ($0.50/share)

PLATFORM DIVERSIFICATION (FEAUM breakdown):
- GPMS: 29%
- Credit: 26%
- Real Estate: 19%
- Private Equity: 12%
- Infrastructure: 9%
- Public Equities: 6%
- "Over 35 investment strategies with more than 100 products with no single product representing more than 8%"

THREE-YEAR TARGET PROGRESS:
- $21B cumulative fundraising target: $7.7B achieved in year 1 (37% of target)
- $70B FEAUM by end 2027: tracking ahead with $40.8B reported + acquisition pipeline
- 2026 fundraising target: $7B
- 2027 fundraising target: $8B
- 2026 FRE target: $225M-$245M ($1.42-$1.54/share)
- 2027 FRE target: $260M-$290M ($1.60-$1.80/share)

CREDIT PLATFORM:
- Record $1.8B of capital raised for Credit in 2025
- Solis acquisition (51% stake): ~$3.5B FEAUM
- "Pro forma for the acquisition, our credit vertical fee-earning AUM is approximately $12.1 billion"

INFRASTRUCTURE:
- Raised ~$2.3B for infrastructure strategies in 2025
- "This was approximately 5x what we raised for infrastructure in 2024"
- Fund V ($2.9B) — largest in LatAm

GPMS:
- "GPMS raised almost $2 billion in 2025"
- WP Global Partners acquisition: $1.8B FEAUM
- "Pro forma for the acquisition, our GPMS fee-earning AUM is approximately $13.6 billion"
- Nearly 40% of GPMS investments in U.S. assets post-WP

REAL ESTATE:
- Over $520M raised in Q4 2025
- RBR acquisition: ~$1.3B FEAUM
- "Largest manager of listed REITs in Brazil with a pro forma fee-earning AUM of approximately $5.7 billion"
- Real Estate strategies now >20% of total FEAUM
- 90% in permanent capital vehicles

ASSET QUALITY & STICKINESS:
- ~90% of FEAUM in vehicles with no or limited redemptions
- 22% ($9.1B) in permanent capital vehicles
- Redemptions decreased ~25% in 2025 vs 2024

CAPITAL ALLOCATION:
- Approved additional 3M share buyback program
- PHL (Patria Partners holding) intends to purchase up to 2.5M PAX shares
- "Summing it all up, we can now purchase up to 7 million shares to return capital to shareholders"
- Quarterly dividend of $0.15/share

M&A STRATEGY:
- $18B of fee-paying AUM through acquisitions targeted over 3-year period
- Three acquisitions announced in Q4 2025 alone (Solis, RBR, WP Global)
- "Acquisitions in very strategically placed" manner
- All three expected accretive to FRE and DE in first year
