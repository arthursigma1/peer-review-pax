# Ghost Report Skeleton — Run4 Action Titles

1. Scale is the dominant valuation lever in alternative asset management, but no single metric qualifies as a statistically stable driver across all multiples
2. Total AUM commands the strongest empirical premium, yet scale alone does not explain why mid-scale specialists trade at peer-level multiples
3. Earnings quality and operational efficiency send consistent directional signals that fall short of statistical thresholds in a heterogeneous 23-firm universe
4. The firms that convert moderate scale into premium valuations share a common pattern: capital-light, high-margin fee streams with permanent capital anchors
5. Credit platform acquisitions have emerged as the dominant mid-scale strategy for improving earnings quality without requiring organic AUM doubling
6. GP-led solutions and minority stakes offer the only observed triple-lever play — simultaneously improving earnings, permanent capital, and efficiency ratios
7. Technology investments correlate with operational metrics at the firm level but lack sufficient cross-sectional coverage to validate as an independent driver
8. Anti-patterns cluster around two failure modes: conglomerate discount from unintegrated acquisitions, and fee-rate dilution from undifferentiated channel expansion
9. Patria's structural discount is primarily a scale phenomenon, but its earnings quality position (above-median DE/share) suggests the discount overstates fundamental weakness
10. The peer evidence supports a phased re-rating path centered on GPMS scaling and selective credit expansion, with technology infrastructure as a prerequisite rather than a catalyst
