This analysis identifies the quantitative drivers of valuation multiples across 24 publicly listed alternative asset managers and translates the findings into an actionable strategic playbook for Patria Investments Limited (NASDAQ: PAX). The study employs Spearman rank correlations, bootstrap confidence intervals, Benjamini-Hochberg multiple testing correction, leave-one-out robustness tests, and partial correlations controlling for scale.

Key Findings

Three stable value drivers form a coherent framework: Scale (Fee-Earning AUM, ρ = +0.765 vs. P/FRE, padj = 0.026) establishes the fee base; Efficiency (FRE Margin, ρ = +0.708 vs. EV/FEAUM, padj = 0.038) determines earnings conversion; and Per-share economics (EPS, ρ = +0.750 vs. EV/FEAUM, padj = 0.041) integrates all earnings sources into the metric that determines equity value. Strategic Implications for Patria Investments

PHL / Board Guidance

Top 5 Value-Creating Principles

1. Protect Q1 FRE Margin as PAX's Primary Competitive Advantage

PAX's 58.9% FRE margin (rank 5/23) is its only top-quartile position on a stable value driver. Every strategic initiative must be evaluated against its margin impact. The Vinci Compass anti-pattern (ANTI-001, ANTI-004) demonstrates that LatAm managers growing without margin discipline destroy value. Linked driver: DVR-003 (avg |ρ| = 0.54, independent efficiency signal).

2. Grow FEAUM through Credit Expansion and Infrastructure Scaling

FEAUM (Q4, rank 19/23) is the strongest overall valuation driver (avg |ρ| = 0.695). Credit expansion (Solis integration plus new products) and infrastructure fund scaling ($2.9B to $4–5B) are the two most viable paths to FEAUM growth without sacrificing FRE margin quality. Linked plays: PLAY-003, PLAY-PE06, PLAY-I03, PLAY-C02.

3. Improve EPS Trajectory through Per-Share Economics Discipline

EPS (Q3, rank 12/16) is the second-strongest value driver (avg |ρ| = 0.729, independent signal). PAX's $0.54 EPS is well below Q1 peers (PGHN CHF 48.83, APO $5.54, HLNE $4.35). Every growth investment must demonstrably contribute to EPS improvement within 24 months. Linked anti-pattern: ANTI-003 (StepStone's negative EPS at scale).

4. Build Permanent Capital to Reduce Fundraising Cyclicality

Permanent capital reduces earnings volatility, extends fee duration, and provides a higher-quality earnings stream that warrants premium multiples. Target 30–40% permanent/long-dated capital within 3 years through open-ended infrastructure and credit vehicles plus Solis CLO book. Linked plays: PLAY-001, PLAY-013, PLAY-C05.

5. Leverage LatAm Scarcity Premium as Geographic Competitive Moat

PAX is one of very few NASDAQ-listed pure-play LatAm alternative managers. Rather than competing with BX/BAM/KKR on global scale, PAX should deepen its positioning as the "first call" for institutional LatAm alternatives allocation. Linked plays: PLAY-007, PLAY-011, PLAY-I04.

Methodology and Statistical Framework

Approach

This analysis employs Spearman rank correlation (ρ) as the primary measure of association between driver metrics and valuation multiples across a universe of 24 publicly listed alternative asset managers (25 listed, BN excluded due to AUM overlap with BAM). Spearman was selected over Pearson for robustness to non-normality, outlier resistance, and appropriateness for small samples (N = 10–19 per correlation).

Multiple regression was explicitly not used due to: insufficient degrees of freedom (14 candidate predictors for ~19 observations), severe multicollinearity (26 driver pairs with |ρ| > 0.7), overfitting risk, and endogeneity concerns.

DVR-001: Fee-Earning AUM (Scale) Stable Driver

Fee-Earning AUM exhibits the strongest and most consistent positive association with valuation multiples. Spearman ρ ranges from +0.63 to +0.76 across three multiples, with average |ρ| = 0.695. The P/FRE correlation (ρ = +0.765, 95% CI [+0.333, +0.942], BH-adjusted p = 0.026) is the only correlation surviving the strictest Bonferroni correction (raw p = 0.0003). Leave-one-out analysis confirms robustness — no single firm drives the result (LOO range 0.112).

DVR-003: FRE Margin Multiple-Specific (EV/FEAUM)

FRE Margin exceeds the ρ > 0.5 threshold for exactly one multiple (EV/FEAUM: ρ = +0.708, BH-adjusted p = 0.038), but not for P/FRE (ρ = +0.404) or P/DE (ρ = +0.515, not significant). FRE Margin retains an independent signal after controlling for scale, though partially attenuated: partial ρ drops from +0.61 to +0.41, indicating ~one-third of the signal is attributable to operating leverage from scale. The remaining +0.41 represents a genuine, independent efficiency signal.

PAX's strongest competitive positioning. FRE margin of 58.9% (rank 5/23, Q1) is comparable to mega-cap platforms despite Q4 scale. This is the single strongest data point supporting PAX's valuation case.

Software and Reproducibility

Spearman rank correlation with tie-handling (average ranks). P-values via t-distribution approximation: t = ρ × √((N-2) / (1-ρ²)), df = N-2. Bootstrap: 1,000 iterations, seed = 42. BH FDR: standard step-up procedure at q = 0.10 across 51 tests. Partial correlations: partial_ρ(X,Y|Z) = (ρXY - ρXZ × ρYZ) / √((1 - ρXZ²) × (1 - ρYZ²)). Leave-one-out: exhaustive recomputation excluding each firm.

Mandatory Disclaimers

Correlation does not imply causation. The Spearman rank correlations reported in this analysis measure the strength and direction of monotonic association between driver metrics and valuation multiples. They do not establish causal relationships. A positive correlation between FEAUM and P/FRE, for example, could reflect that (a) larger AUM causes higher multiples, (b) higher multiples enable cheaper capital and faster AUM growth, (c) both are driven by an unobserved third factor (e.g., brand quality, founder reputation), or (d) some combination of these mechanisms.

Survivorship bias: universe consists of currently listed firms only. The universe of 25 firms includes only alternative asset managers that are publicly listed as of FY2025. Firms that were acquired (e.g., Sculptor/Rithm), delisted (e.g., Petershill Partners), or remained private are excluded. This creates survivorship bias: the observed correlations reflect the characteristics of firms that have survived as public entities, which may systematically differ from the full population of alt-asset managers.

Point-in-time limitation: conditions as of most recent data collection period. All data reflects conditions as of FY2025 (calendar year ended December 31, 2025, or nearest fiscal year equivalent). Valuation multiples are computed using market capitalizations from December 2025 through March 2026 (varying by tier). Market conditions, interest rates, and investor sentiment at this specific point in time influence both driver metrics and valuation multiples. Results may not generalize to different market regimes.

Small-N limitation: N ≈ 25 limits statistical power; findings generate hypotheses, not definitive causal claims. The largest full-sample correlation (P/FRE) includes N = 19 firms. The consistent sub-sample includes only N = 9 firms. At these sample sizes, statistical power is limited: even true correlations of moderate strength (ρ = 0.4) may not achieve statistical significance, and apparent significant correlations may reflect sampling noise. The four BH-significant findings should be treated as well-supported hypotheses warranting further investigation, not as established facts.

Endogeneity: several drivers may be simultaneously caused by and causally related to valuation multiples. AUM growth, fundraising intensity, and fee-related earnings are all influenced by a firm's market valuation. High-multiple firms attract more investor capital (cheaper equity issuance, greater LP confidence), which mechanically increases AUM and earnings. This bidirectional causality means that observed correlations likely overstate the unidirectional effect of drivers on multiples. Instrumental variable or natural experiment approaches would be needed to estimate causal effects, but are infeasible at this sample size.

FRE definition heterogeneity: FRE is non-GAAP; firm-specific definitions vary; measurement error is present. Fee-Related Earnings is a non-GAAP metric with material definitional differences across firms. Key variations include: pre-tax vs. after-tax treatment; inclusion or exclusion of equity-based compensation; treatment of transaction, advisory, and monitoring fees; and European equivalents that use IFRS-based operating profit proxies (e.g., EQT's "Fee-related EBITDA," CVC's "Management Fee Earnings," Bridgepoint's "Underlying EBITDA"). These definitional differences introduce measurement error that attenuates correlations and may bias comparisons.

INFERRED claims policy: Claims marked as INFERRED require hedged language. In this report: (1) BX ~50% private wealth market share — "appears to represent approximately 50%" (company IR estimate, not independently verified); (2) APO "parallel bank" framing — "company IR narrative, not independently verified"; the verified data point is $228B origination volume.
