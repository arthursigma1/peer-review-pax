## 4. Gaps and Recommendations

### 4.1 Identified Gaps

1. **Fiscal Year Misalignment:** HLNE and STEP have March 31 fiscal year ends vs. December 31 for all other peers. Their 10-K filings cover periods ending March 31, 2025. For calendar year 2025 alignment, we rely on their interim quarterly filings (FQ3 FY2026, ending December 31, 2025). This is a structural gap that cannot be eliminated but is adequately mitigated by quarterly disclosures.

2. **FY2025 20-F Not Yet Filed for PAX and VINP:** Both foreign private issuers file 20-F annually (typically April-May). The FY2025 20-F for both companies is expected in April-May 2026. Current coverage relies on quarterly 6-K filings and earnings releases, which provide sufficient interim data.

3. **Currency Comparability (VINP):** Vinci Compass reports exclusively in BRL. Converting to USD for peer comparison introduces exchange rate variability. The R$354.1B AUM translates to approximately $60B USD, but this figure fluctuates with BRL/USD rates. Recommend using constant-currency growth rates where VINP provides them.

4. **Scale Disparity:** The peer group spans a wide AUM range: BAM ($1T+) and ARES ($622B) are dramatically larger than PAX ($52.6B) and VINP (~$60B). This may limit the usefulness of absolute-value comparisons. Ratio-based metrics (FRE margins, fee rates, AUM growth rates) will be more meaningful for benchmarking.

5. **Non-GAAP Metric Definitions:** Each company defines FRE, DE, and related non-GAAP metrics slightly differently. Component definitions vary regarding inclusion/exclusion of transaction fees, realized carry, fund-level expenses, and equity-based compensation. Metric harmonization notes should be documented during Stage P1.

6. **Limited Third-Party Analyst Coverage:** Publicly available sell-side research is limited to rating summaries and price targets. Detailed analyst models with peer comparisons are behind paywalls (Bloomberg Terminal, Capital IQ). The industry reports (Bain, McKinsey, Preqin) partially compensate by providing market-level benchmarks.

7. **STEP Advisory vs. Management Fee Mix:** StepStone derives a significant portion of revenue from advisory/consulting fees rather than traditional management fees, making direct FRE comparison with pure-play asset managers (BAM, ARES, OWL) less straightforward.

### 4.2 Recommendations

1. **Proceed to Stage P1** with all 7 companies. All meet minimum sufficiency requirements.

2. **Prioritize ratio-based metrics** over absolute values given the 20x AUM range across the peer group.

3. **Create a non-GAAP reconciliation table** during Stage P1 to document how each company defines FRE, FRE margin, and DE, enabling apples-to-apples comparison.

4. **Use calendar year 2025 as the primary comparison period** (January-December 2025), using interim filings for HLNE and STEP to align with the December 31 reporting date used by other peers.

5. **Convert VINP BRL metrics to USD** using period-end spot rates for balance sheet items and average period rates for income statement items, noting the methodology explicitly.

6. **Monitor for FY2025 20-F filings** from PAX and VINP (expected April-May 2026). These will provide audited full-year data to validate quarterly figures used in the interim.
