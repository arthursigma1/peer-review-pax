## Section 1: Operational scalability — not scale itself — is the dominant valuation signal across listed alternative asset managers

*Lead finding: G&A/FEAUM is the only stable value driver (ρ = −0.61 on P/FRE, ρ = −0.51 on P/DE; N=19). Total AUM and FEAUM are correlated with P/FRE and P/DE but as multiple-specific signals, not stable ones. The market distinguishes between managers that grow fee-earning assets and managers that do so without proportionally growing overhead.*

---

## Section 2: Earnings quality confirms the efficiency signal — distributable earnings per share is the second pillar of durable premium valuations

*Cross-run finding: DE/share was the stable driver in the prior analysis (ρ = 0.857 on EV/FEAUM, N=9). Both signals are valid and complementary: G&A/FEAUM measures whether a platform can scale profitably; DE/share measures whether that profitability reaches shareholders. Firms that achieve both command the most durable premiums.*

## Section 3: Most managers face a structural tension between scale and efficiency — a small number have resolved it by changing the rules

*The Scale-Efficiency Paradox: Q1 on AUM/FEAUM scale drivers and Q1 on G&A/FEAUM efficiency are anti-correlated across the universe (ρ ≈ −0.55). The exceptions — Apollo (insurance float), Blue Owl (permanent capital by design), StepStone (advisory scope economies), CVC (post-IPO discipline) — did not optimize within the paradox. They restructured the business model to make the tradeoff disappear.*

---

## Section 4: Permanent capital is the most reliable mechanism for escaping the scale-efficiency paradox — but each implementation path has distinct operational prerequisites

*Platform plays: PLAY-001 through PLAY-003 document three validated paths to permanent capital G&A efficiency — insurance-linked origination (Apollo/KKR), purpose-built perpetual structures (Blue Owl), and post-IPO reporting discipline (CVC). Each requires specific sequencing: systems integration before distribution expansion, data infrastructure before product proliferation.*

## Section 5: FRE growth signals outperform absolute FRE levels — the market prices trajectory, not current earnings, across credit and PE franchises

*DVR-002 (FRE Growth YoY) shows ρ = −0.55 on P/DE: counterintuitively, high FRE growth firms trade at lower P/DE. The mechanism: high-growth platforms have not yet reached steady-state margins, so earnings quality lags revenue quality. PLAY-004 and PLAY-005 document how insurance-integrated credit and high-fee regional monopolies (LatAm) sustain FRE growth with improving, not declining, efficiency.*

## Section 6: Asset class economics explain the variance within each driver — infrastructure and GP stakes are the most G&A-efficient sub-types; multi-franchise roll-ups are the least

*Vertical findings: Infrastructure specialists (ANTIN, 59% FRE margin at €33B AUM) and GP stakes (OWL, STEP) achieve the lowest G&A/FEAUM ratios in the universe through structurally capital-light models. Traditional multi-strategy PE (CG, RPC, RF) consistently occupies Q4, driven by duplicated cost structures across strategies and geographies.*

## Section 7: The cautionary cases are more instructive than the exemplars — value destruction follows predictable operational mechanisms

*Anti-patterns: ANTI-001 through ANTI-005 document five documented mechanisms of G&A inefficiency: roll-up without back-office consolidation (RPC), balance-sheet cost drag in hybrid AM models (RF), geographic fragmentation without shared services (CG), efficiency without growth (CG stagnation trap), and specialist scale constraints (ANTIN infrastructure concentration). Each has a specific trigger, timeline, and recovery path.*

## Section 8: The peer evidence suggests that the highest-impact efficiency levers for LatAm-scale managers involve structural product decisions, not cost-cutting

*Target Company Lens: Based on the peer evidence, structural changes to FEAUM composition — specifically GP stakes scaling (GPMS) and open-end infrastructure vehicles — appear to address the G&A efficiency gap more effectively than operational cost reduction alone. Peers that achieved similar transitions did so over 3–5 year horizons with front-loaded operational investment.*

## Section 9: Several cautionary patterns in the peer universe suggest conditions under which efficiency improvement attempts destroy rather than create value

*Anti-patterns for PAX context: The peer evidence is consistent with three structural traps that affect mid-scale managers attempting to improve G&A efficiency: premature product proliferation before platform consolidation, geographic expansion that fragments cost structures, and acquisition-driven AUM growth that adds cost before integration.*
