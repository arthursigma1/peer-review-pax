### Gaps Identified

1. **20-F for FY2025 not yet available.** The FY2024 20-F (filed May 2025) is the most recent annual report. The FY2025 20-F is expected in April-May 2026. This is a timing gap, not a data gap -- quarterly 6-K filings and earnings releases provide interim coverage.

2. **CVM (Brazil regulator) filings not directly accessible.** Patria's Brazilian subsidiary regulatory filings with CVM are in Portuguese and not readily available through English-language search. The SEC filings provide equivalent disclosure for U.S. investors.

3. **Full-length sell-side analyst reports are paywalled.** Only Goldman Sachs rating/PT summary is available publicly. Detailed analyst models and assumptions from other covering brokerages (BTG Pactual, etc.) are behind paywalls. The consensus rating aggregate partially compensates.

4. **Private Equity Fund VII performance data is limited.** Fund VII (2023 vintage, $1.2B) is closed but detailed deployment and performance data is not yet public beyond aggregate PE segment reporting.

5. **Patria Private Equity Trust (LSE: PPET) annual report** is a related but distinct entity. Available but not included as primary source since it covers a specific investment trust, not the parent company strategy.

### Source Quality Notes

- **Regulatory filings (20-F, 6-K)** carry the highest reliability: legally mandated disclosures with material misstatement liability under SEC rules.
- **Earnings call transcripts** are particularly valuable for this company because the Q&A portions reveal management candor on competitive dynamics, integration challenges, and fundraising pipeline -- areas where prepared remarks may be less forthcoming.
- **Bloomberg reporting** provides strong independent validation, particularly on infrastructure and credit strategy moves.
- **AIIB approval** of Fund V investment provides unique institutional validation that Patria's infrastructure platform meets multilateral development bank standards.
